F&O Best Practices

📐 Position Sizing ▼
Never risk more than 1–2% of total capital on a single trade.
Define your max loss in rupees before entry — not as a % you calculate after the fact.
Keep F&O capital separate from your core portfolio. Treat it as a fixed speculation budget.
Deploy max 20–30% of F&O capital in any single week.
🛑 Stop Loss Discipline ▼
Define your exit before entry — never after the trade goes against you.
A 30–50% drop in premium is a common SL zone for long options.
Never average down on a losing option — time decay accelerates losses the longer you hold.
If you've hit your monthly max loss, stop trading for the rest of the month.
⚖️ Risk : Reward ▼
Minimum 1:1.5 R:R to be viable long-term; 1:2 or better is ideal.
Even with a 40% win rate, a 1:2 R:R is profitable — the math works in your favour.
Don't take trades where R:R is less than 1:1 — the edge isn't there.
⏱️ Entry Timing ▼
Avoid buying options on expiry day unless scalping — theta decay is brutal.
Buy with at least 5–7 days to expiry for positional trades.
Enter on pullbacks to support, not at breakout peaks — you pay a far better premium.
🧠 Mindset ▼
A loss within your pre-defined limit is a good trade — execution mattered, not the outcome.
Markets are random in the short term. Edge plays out over 20–30 trades, not one.
The goal is consistency over sample size — not one big win that changes everything.
